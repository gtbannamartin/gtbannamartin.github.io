NSF TechAccess: AI-Ready America — Up to $3,000,000 per Hub

The National Science Foundation's TechAccess: AI-Ready America program funds state- and territory-level coordination hubs that expand access to AI literacy, workforce training, and business adoption. Each hub receives up to $1 million per year for three years. Unlike traditional research grants, these hubs are designed as operational coordination infrastructure — eligible applicants include coalitions of nonprofits, community colleges, workforce boards, and local governments. Round 1 is open now.

Eligibility Checklist
  • U.S. nonprofit, community college, workforce board, or government entity
  • Focus on AI literacy, workforce training, or business AI adoption at state/territory level
  • Coalition or hub structure — single organizations can apply as hub coordinators
  • Capacity to coordinate across multiple stakeholder types (education, business, government)
  • Commitment to equity and access for underserved communities preferred
Application Tip: NSF is funding operational hubs, not research projects. Lead with your coordination capacity and existing stakeholder relationships — not your technology. Organizations with documented community partnerships in AI literacy or workforce development are positioned to lead or co-lead hub applications.
