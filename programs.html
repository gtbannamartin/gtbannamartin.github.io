Pillar I — Youth AI & STEM Education

Building the Pipeline That Doesn't Exist

Free, high-quality AI and STEM education for 200+ students across Chicagoland since 2021 — with a focused commitment to young Black and Brown students who are systematically prepared to consume technology but never to build it. This pillar has already produced Yale, Northwestern, and University of Chicago alumni, placed students into NIH-funded AI research, and generated a CBS News-featured $40,000 scholarship.

Pillar II — Workforce & Economic Mobility

Credentialed Pathways Into Careers That Pay

The global cybersecurity workforce gap is nearly 4 million professionals — and the communities most excluded from filling it are exactly the communities DJMP serves. As one of only 863 official Fortinet Academic Partners globally, DJMP delivers free, industry-grade cybersecurity training to high school students, opportunity youth, returning citizens, veterans, and adults. Five certification levels. Free exam vouchers. Direct pathways to $80K+ starting salaries.

Certifications: FCF → FCA → FCP → FCSS → FCX · Count toward CompTIA CEUs and ISC2 CPE credits
Populations: Opportunity youth 18–24 · Veterans · Justice-impacted · Displaced workers
Digital Navigator: Community members trained and compensated as frontline technology guides

Civic AI Infrastructure · Live Proof of Concept

PathwayAI Isn't a Program.
It's the Evidence That Everything Else Works.

PathwayAI is DJMP's first autonomous civic AI platform — three AI agents powered by Google Gemini continuously monitoring ten federal databases, connecting marginalized residents to publicly funded education and workforce training. It is not a service offering. It is the proof-of-concept artifact that demonstrates TCAF operating in production, the foundation of Martin Pieters' doctoral research, and the system that gave DJMP the credibility to apply for $1.5M from Google.org.

→Three autonomous agents · Google Gemini · LangChain · FastAPI · Kubernetes · Twilio SMS
→Ten federal databases: BLS · O*NET · WIOA · CareerOneStop · USAJobs · Federal Student Aid · 4 more
→Delivery: Web · Mobile · SMS — no resident excluded by device access
→Google.org Impact Challenge application submitted March 2026 — $1.5M over 36 months
